# Licensing Model

NAM is **not an open source project**.

The source is available for transparency, evaluation, and collaboration — but **use is permissioned**.

This document explains how licensing works at a conceptual level.  
It does not replace the actual license terms.

---

## Source-Available, Not Open Source

NAM follows a **source-available licensing model**.

This means:
- You can read the code
- You can understand how the system works
- You can evaluate its architecture and behavior

It does *not* mean:
- Unrestricted use
- Free commercial deployment
- Automatic rights to modify or redistribute

---

## Intentional Use

NAM is designed to be used **intentionally**, not casually embedded.

Any use of NAM — including:
- Evaluation
- Prototyping
- Internal experimentation
- Commercial deployment

…requires **explicit permission and an appropriate license**.

This is a deliberate choice to:
- Protect architectural integrity
- Prevent misuse or misrepresentation
- Ensure deployments reflect the system’s design intent

---

## Evaluation and Non-Commercial Use

Evaluation licenses may be granted for:
- Technical assessment
- Research exploration
- Proof-of-concept work
- Partner evaluation

These licenses are:
- Time-bounded
- Scope-limited
- Non-transferable

They do not grant rights to deploy NAM in production environments.

---

## Commercial Use

Commercial use of NAM requires a **separate commercial license**.

Commercial licensing terms may vary based on:
- Deployment scale
- Use case
- Embedded vs standalone usage
- Support and collaboration requirements

Commercial licenses are negotiated directly.

---

## Modifications and Derivative Work

Modifying NAM does not grant ownership or usage rights.

Unless explicitly agreed:
- Derivative works remain subject to the original license
- Modified versions may not be redistributed
- Architectural constraints must be preserved

Forking for learning or evaluation is permitted.  
Forking to compete or bypass licensing is not.

---

## Future Licensing Evolution

The licensing model for NAM **may evolve over time**.

This may include:
- Dual licensing
- Open-core components
- Domain-specific licensing models
- Broader community access

Any such changes will be explicit and forward-looking.  
There are no retroactive rights.

---

## Why This Model Exists

NAM explores a new category of system.

This licensing model exists to:
- Enable serious evaluation
- Support responsible deployment
- Maintain architectural coherence
- Allow sustainable development

It is not intended to block use — only to ensure use is aligned.
